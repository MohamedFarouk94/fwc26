How does this work?

Every time you hit Simulate, the entire 2026 FIFA World Cup, with all matches from group stage to the final, plays out inside the machine. For each game, an AI model trained on over 150 years of international football results estimates how many goals each team is expected to score, based on their strength, attacking ability, defensive vulnerability, and home advantage. The model then rolls statistically weighted dice to turn those expectations into a real scoreline. One click is one tournament, one story, one of billions of ways the beautiful game could play out.

For the full technical breakdown, the rating system, the xG model, the score generator, and the backtesting on past World Cups, see my Kaggle notebook.
